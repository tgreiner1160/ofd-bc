PERS 1-0 — CLASS SPECIFICATIONS AND SPECIALTY CATEGORIES
----------------------------------------------------------

**Rank structure and civil service:** The Fire Chief is a civil service position selected through competitive testing and personal interviews, confirmed by the Mayor. All officer and firefighter positions are civil service, with promotions through competitive examination — exactly what you are doing now.

**Battalion Chief responsibilities:** As a BC you supervise and direct the operations of companies within your battalion. You are responsible for personnel management, training oversight, scene command, and administrative functions for your assigned area. You report directly to the Operations Assistant Chief for your shift.

**Specialty categories:** OFD maintains specialty-trained personnel in areas including HazMat, Technical Rescue, Dive Rescue, Swift Water Rescue, and Paramedic. Personnel in specialty categories receive specialty pay per the union contract. The BC must be familiar with the specialty capabilities assigned to companies in their battalion.


PERS 3-0 — CODE OF CONDUCT AND FIREFIGHTER CODE OF ETHICS
-----------------------------------------------------------

**Core obligations:** Every OFD employee must: report at their assigned hour and place ready to competently perform their duties; complete their tour of duty unless authorized absence is properly documented; be available and mentally and physically prepared for emergency assignments at all times while on duty; and conduct themselves professionally in all interactions with the public and fellow employees.

**Discipline process — FFBOR (Firefighter Bill of Rights):** Article 6 of the union contract establishes the Firefighter Bill of Rights. Before any member is interrogated as part of a formal investigation, they must receive **48-hour written notice** of the nature of the investigation, the charges being investigated, and the time and place of the interrogation. The interrogation is **limited to 2 hours** with a **15-minute break at the midpoint and a 45-minute break** if extended beyond 2 hours. The member has the right to a union representative present. No member may be coerced to waive these rights.

**Levels of discipline:** The progressive discipline system begins with an oral reprimand (documented in writing), then written reprimand, then suspension (up to 20 working days for most offenses, up to 32 working days for serious offenses per the contract), then termination. **A written reprimand is removed from the personnel file after 2 years with no further discipline. A suspension record is removed after 3 years.**

**Just cause standard:** All discipline must be based on just cause — the offense must be proven, the rule violated must be reasonable and communicated, the penalty must be proportional, and the member must have been warned that the conduct could result in discipline. Failure to follow just cause leads to overturned discipline in arbitration.

**BC role in discipline:** The BC initiates the disciplinary process when a violation occurs. Document the violation immediately and thoroughly — what happened, who was involved, date, time, location, witnesses. Submit the documentation to the AC for review. Never bypass the chain of command in the discipline process.


PERS 3-1 — EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY POLICY
-------------------------------------------------

**EEO principle:** All employees must support the principles of equal opportunity. Discrimination based on race, sex, age, religion, national origin, disability, or any other protected class is prohibited. All personnel are entitled to work in an environment free from harassment and discrimination.

**Supervisor accountability:** Supervisors (including BCs and Captains) are held personally accountable for enforcing EEO provisions as they relate to employees under their supervision. If a supervisor witnesses or is made aware of discriminatory conduct, they must take immediate corrective action and report it through the chain of command.

**Complaint procedure:** Employees who believe they have experienced discrimination or harassment may file a complaint with their BC, the AC, or directly with Human Resources. The BC who receives a complaint must forward it to the AC and HR immediately — do not attempt to investigate an EEO complaint at the BC level without HR involvement.


PERS 3-2 — SPECIAL DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES FOR LOSS OF DRIVER'S LICENSE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Impact of license loss:** A valid driver's license is required for all OFD operational personnel. Loss of driving privileges — for any reason including DUI, excessive violations, or administrative suspension — must be reported to the member's BC immediately. Failure to report is an additional violation.

**BC responsibility:** Upon notification of license loss, the BC notifies the AC immediately. The member may be restricted from driving apparatus and assigned to limited duty, placed on administrative leave, or otherwise accommodated depending on the circumstances. The BC documents the notification and any actions taken.

**Return to driving:** Personnel may not return to driving duties until their license is fully restored and they have notified their supervisor and received clearance from administration.


PERS 3-4 — CITY OF OMAHA ALCOHOL AND DRUG POLICY
--------------------------------------------------

**Zero tolerance on duty:** No OFD member may report for duty under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance. Prescription medications that impair performance must be disclosed to the BC — the member may be restricted from driving or operational duties while on those medications.

**Testing requirements:** OFD members are subject to drug and alcohol testing in the following circumstances: pre-employment, reasonable suspicion, post-accident (any vehicle accident involving city property), return-to-duty after a positive test, and random testing per department protocol.

**BC's reasonable suspicion authority:** A BC who observes signs of impairment (slurred speech, unsteady gait, odor of alcohol, unusual behavior) has the authority — and the obligation — to remove the member from duty and initiate reasonable suspicion testing. Document observations in writing immediately. Do not allow an impaired member to drive or perform operational duties under any circumstances. Contact the AC immediately.

**Consequences of positive test:** A positive result for drugs or alcohol results in mandatory referral to the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) and possible disciplinary action up to and including termination, depending on the circumstances and prior history.


PERS 4-0 — LEAVE PROVISIONS — LITIGATION / JURY DUTY / ELECTION DUTY
----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Jury duty:** Members summoned for jury duty receive full pay for the duration of jury service. They must notify their BC as soon as they receive the jury summons. Members are expected to report for their shift on days when jury duty does not conflict — if released from jury duty early enough to report for duty, they should do so.

**Subpoenas and litigation:** Members subpoenaed to testify in their official capacity (as a witness in a case related to their OFD duties) are considered on-duty for purposes of compensation. Members subpoenaed in a personal capacity are responsible for their own representation and time — consult the union if there is uncertainty.

**Election duty:** Members serving as election judges receive approved leave and are compensated per city policy.


PERS 4-1 — LEAVE PROVISIONS — ANNUAL / FUNERAL / MILITARY / IOD / SICK LEAVE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Annual (vacation) leave:** Full-time operations personnel accrue annual leave based on years of service per the union contract — **120 hours per year for members with under 15 years of service, 240 hours per year for members with 15 or more years.** Leave requests are submitted to the Captain and approved by the BC subject to minimum staffing requirements.

**Funeral leave:** Members receive up to three consecutive scheduled shifts for the death of an immediate family member without charge to sick or annual leave. Extended funeral leave may be charged to annual leave or sick leave at the member's request.

**Military leave:** Members on active military duty receive leave per federal USERRA requirements and state law. Military leave must be documented and reported to HR. The BC ensures coverage is arranged per minimum staffing requirements.

**IOD leave:** Injury-on-duty leave is governed by Article 21 of the contract — **48-hour filing, 45-day determination, up to 365 days of paid leave.** See ADM 3-2-1 for full IOD procedures.

**Sick leave:** Accrues at **5.7 hours per pay period** for operations personnel. Used for personal illness, injury, or medical appointments. Call-in before **0600** (or 0730 per some provisions). The BC monitors sick leave patterns and addresses suspected abuse through documented counseling and progressive discipline as needed.


PERS 4-2 — FAMILY MEDICAL LEAVE ACT (FMLA)
--------------------------------------------

**Federal entitlement:** The FMLA entitles eligible employees to up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for qualifying reasons — serious health condition of the employee or immediate family member, birth or adoption of a child, or qualifying military exigency.

**Eligibility:** Must have worked for the city for at least 12 months and at least 1,250 hours in the past 12 months. All OFD suppression personnel typically meet this threshold well before FMLA issues arise.

**BC responsibility:** When a member requests leave that may qualify for FMLA, the BC must notify HR immediately — FMLA designation is HR's responsibility, not the BC's. Do not deny leave that may be FMLA-qualifying without HR guidance. Interference with FMLA rights is a federal violation and exposes the city and the supervisor to liability.


PERS 5-0 — EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP)
----------------------------------------------

**What EAP provides:** Confidential counseling, referral, and support services for OFD personnel and their immediate families. Services cover mental health concerns, substance abuse, financial issues, family problems, and work-related stress. Access is confidential — supervisors are not informed of self-referrals.

**Mandatory vs. voluntary EAP:** Personnel may self-refer voluntarily at any time. Mandatory EAP referrals are made by the BC or administration following a positive drug or alcohol test, after a critical incident, or when a behavioral concern is observed. Mandatory EAP participation may be a condition of continued employment.

**BC role:** The BC is not a counselor. If a member shows signs of distress, behavioral changes, or performance issues that may have an underlying cause, the BC's role is to address the performance or behavior issue and, where appropriate, inform the member of EAP resources. Never diagnose — focus on observable job performance.


PERS 5-1 — PEER SUPPORT PROGRAM AND CISD
------------------------------------------

**Critical Incident Stress Management (CISM):** After traumatic incidents (LODD, pediatric deaths, mass casualty events, incidents involving known victims), OFD provides CISM services through its Peer Support Team. The Peer Support Team consists of trained OFD personnel who provide informal peer-to-peer support and facilitate formal debriefs.

**CISD (Critical Incident Stress Debriefing):** A structured group debriefing facilitated by trained peer supporters and, when needed, mental health professionals. CISD is not an investigation — nothing said in a CISD may be used in a disciplinary proceeding. Participation is encouraged but voluntary.

**BC responsibility:** After a traumatic incident, the BC should assess their crew for signs of acute stress reaction and ensure access to peer support is offered. The BC notifies the CISM coordinator if a debriefing is needed. The BC also has access to EAP and peer support for their own needs — being a BC does not make you immune to critical incident stress.


PERS 8-0 — AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES ACT (ADA)
--------------------------------------------------

**Reasonable accommodation:** OFD is required to provide reasonable accommodations to qualified employees with disabilities unless doing so creates an undue hardship. Accommodations might include modified duties, equipment modifications, or schedule adjustments.

**Interactive process:** When a member requests an accommodation or the BC becomes aware of a potential disability-related limitation, the city initiates the "interactive process" — a documented conversation between the employer and employee to identify appropriate accommodations. The BC participates in this process in coordination with HR.

**Essential functions:** The essential functions of a firefighter/paramedic position (responding to emergencies, wearing SCBA, operating apparatus, performing physical rescues) must be performed with or without reasonable accommodation. If a member cannot perform essential functions with any reasonable accommodation, the ADA does not require retention in that position — this is a complex determination made by HR and legal counsel, not the BC independently.
