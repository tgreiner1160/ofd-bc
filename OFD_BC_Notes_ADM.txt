GENERAL INFORMATION “Orders” within the Omaha Fire Department (OFD) shall be classified as one of the following:    General Order    Bulletin    Personnel Order    Assignment Transfer Other communications within the OFD shall be classified as one of the following:    Interdepartmental Communication (OFD 25)    Interoffice Memorandum (OFD 26)    Significant Event Report    Email Official Documentation:    Company Daybook    Medic Daybook FIRE DEPARTMENT ORDERS The effective date of all “Orders” - General Orders, Bulletins, Personnel Orders or Assignment Transfers - will be the date of origin unless otherwise stated. All Orders will be distributed in an electronic format via PowerDMS. A signed copy will no longer be maintained in the office of the Fire Chief as PowerDMS appropriately archives each document’s history with digital signatures.

--- ADM 2-0 ---
Orders General Orders These are orders that effect procedures or policies and apply to all members of the Fire Department. These orders shall continue in effect indefinitely, unless amended or superseded by a new order, Written SOP Material. The Fire Chief or an Assistant Chief usually issue general Orders but when issued by any other Chief Officer, they shall be approved by the Fire Chief. Bulletins These orders shall be used to provide information to all Fire Department personnel and generally are of a lesser importance than a General Order. Bulletins may contain procedure or policy changes that shall be adhered to until notified otherwise. Any Chief Officer may issue bulletins. Those Bulletins which contain procedure or policy changes may be incorporated into the Standard Operating Procedures. Personnel Orders and Assignment Transfers These will address all changes of status, movement of personnel or scheduled assignments. Personnel Orders/Assignment Transfers shall be used in all cases involving a change in Shift or Bureau for any personnel within the Fire Department. These changes may be of either a temporary or permanent nature and shall include appointments, promotions, transfers, assignments, resignations, retirements, in-service or special duty school, death notifications, etc. NOTE: When there is a conflict in written policies or procedures, (The SOP, General Order or Bulletin) the written policy or procedure with the most current date shall be adhered to. PowerDMS Procedures Power DMS is a web-based document management system used by OFD to manage Orders to include Bulletins, General Orders, Personnel Orders, Assignment Transfers and SOPs. Please refer to the current PowerDMS Basic User Guide found under the help menu for detailed information on how to navigate within the PowerDMS system. Omaha Fire Department Orders are very important to the day to day operations and the efficiency of the OFD therefore, all personnel must read and sign each order that is assigned in PowerDMS within two weeks of issue. Exceptions to this rule would include an employee being off on scheduled leave, IOD etc. Personnel are not expected to log in to Power DMS while off duty to read and review policies. Failure to read and sign assigned documents within the two week deadline are subject to disciplinary action as outlined in Article 6 of the CBA and PERS 3-0 Code of Conduct & Firefighter Code of Ethics. Supervisors must make sure personnel are abiding by this directive. Battalion Chiefs will monitor the entire Battalion, counsel when needed and forward information to Fire Management if discipline is required. Failure to follow up is subject to discipline.
FIRE DEPARTMENT COMMUNICATIONS Department Communications are of four different forms: 1. Interdepartmental Communication (OFD 25) shall be used to communicate official or personal information through the chain of command. This information is generally of limited distribution and shall be retained and filed. 2. Interoffice Memorandum (OFD 26) shall be used to communicate information with other City Departments 3. Significant Event Report – see next section for more information 4. Email shall be used for official communication. Any emails sent to the OmaF Everyone and/or OmaF All Sworn email distribution groups must be approved in advance by the Fire Chief or their designee. All written letters/correspondences sent via email that need to go up the chain of command shall be sent to your direct supervisor only. Do not Cc your chain of command. When sending communications via email, the supervisor shall forward the email to the next person in the chain of command and also Cc the person who sent the email to the supervisor. This process will continue each step up the chain of command and will provide documentation that the email was forwarded. (Example: FF sends an email to Captain, then Captain forwards email to BC and Cc the FF, after receiving the email the BC forwards the email to AC and Cc Captain, etc.) List the topic and your chain of command on the subject line. The last name on the subject line should be the individual the letter is intended for. Example: To:         Capt Smith Subject: Furlough hours -- Capt Smith, BC Jones, AC Walters, FC Young Significant Event Reports (SER) OFD Battalion Chiefs, Captains or Lead Medics shall complete a SER to be disseminated via email to inform Fire Management of emergency incidents of significance or that garner media attention and they should also provide a summary of OFD action at a large civic event, such as the CWS. A SER shall be sent via email to the OmaF Significant Event Reports mailbox (OmaF_SER@cityofomaha.org). The SER will be completed as soon as possible after the event takes place. This will give Fire Management advanced notice if the media or others would like information on the event. Significant Event Reports shall be completed for all events of major interest that occur during your shift. Examples of what may constitute a significant or major event:      Threats or acts of violence toward an OFD member (i.e. citizen physically assaulting OFD personnel)      “Help a firefighter” call

Email – Limited distribution OFFICIAL DOCUMENTATION Company Day Book The Company Day Book shall be used by the company officer to record information about any type incident (fire, medical or other). The company officer shall also record all information that good judgment would indicate needs to be recorded into the record of the Company Day Book. Policy The Company Day Book is an official and legal document of the Omaha Fire Department. For this reason, complete and accurate entries into the Company Day Book are essential. The information contained in the Company Day Book is intended for internal Fire Department use only. Fire Department personnel may review and use this information as long as it is for internal fire Department use, Example: (check on - working out of class dates, call back times and dates, personnel on duty or off/running extra/at school, etc.). The use of information contained in a Company Day Book for other than internal Fire Department use is strictly prohibited. All requests for information contained in a Company Day Book by anyone, (fire Department personnel or civilians), for reasons other than internal Fire Department use shall be referred to the Assistant Fire Chief in charge of Fire Prevention. The Company Day Book information will ONLY be released with the approval of City Legal Department, Fire Chief, or the Assistant Fire Chief. The City Legal Department and Fire Chief will determine what information can be released from a Company Day Book and how and by whom that information will be released. The Company Day Book information will ONLY be released once the following steps are completed:      Confirmation of Subpoena or Public Documents request      Approval from the City Legal Department      Approval of the Fire Chief or Designee All completed Company Day Books shall be given to the appropriate Battalion Chief who will turn them in to the SWD Battalion Chief for storage. * PowerDMS master administrator(s) is/are those personnel assigned to manage the PowerDMS SOP Assignments and Annual Review The entire SOP library has been sorted, and the responsibility for each SOP has been assigned to a specific position/assignment. Assigned documents will arrive via PowerDMS with a predetermined workflow in place. When updating an SOP, all changes shall be formatted in red font. During each update process, the previous red updates shall be changed to black font so only the new updates will be in red font. General Orders/Bulletins If needed, an updated version of the SOP shall be completed in a timely fashion following the posting of a GO/Bulletin by the appropriately assigned personnel. The SOP Coordinators will monitor GO/Bulletins for timely changes. If changes do not occur within two weeks, the SOP Coordinators shall contact the originator to assist in the update. The Fire Chief shall be advised when the SOP is not being adequately updated. General Maintenance and Revisions Anyone on the Omaha Fire Department may send an email to the “OmaF SOP Coordinator” address via their chain of command upon recognition of a necessary update. The SOP Coordinators will be able to make simple changes immediately by contacting the Power DMS master administrator. This is for simple changes only, i.e., phone number , form number, station or apparatus number, grammatical errors, missed revisions from previous GO’s/bulletins, contradictions, and non-procedural changes. Suggesting a Change to an SOP Department employees are encouraged to suggest changes to this manual. A written statement of the suggested changes is to be submitted electronically to the Omaha Fire Department SOP Coordinator using an OFD-25. The following shall be included:    The number and title of the instruction.    The procedure/policy for which changes are proposed    The reasons for the change    The proposed wording of the revision Suggestions shall be reviewed by each supervisor within the employee’s chain of command prior to forwarding to the Omaha Fire Department SOP Coordinator. If it is determined that significant changes are needed, the appropriate personnel will be assigned a “work flow” within PowerDMS. The SOP Coordinators will assist if needed. ADMINISTRATION 3-0 NEWS MEDIA & PUBLIC INFORMATION POLICY REVISION HISTORY REVISION #   REVISION DESCRIPTION            DATE REVISED   AUTHOR        REVIEWED BY     APPROVED BY 1             Periodic Revision            Nov 2015     McCaw 897       McEvoy 680     McCormick 638 PIO duties, Significant Event 2                                          Jul 2017     McCaw 897       McCaw 897        Olsen 640 Reports & General Info PURPOSE To develop complete, accurate, relevant, and timely information regarding public safety and issues of general interest for the community at large. To serve as a guide for a department spokesperson when dealing with the news media at the scene of emergency incidents and for releasing information through the Fire Prevention & Public Education Division. The Public Information Officer (PIO) is directly responsible to the Fire Chief. DEFINITIONS Public Information - Information that may be of interest to the general public regarding policy, procedures or events involving the department or other newsworthy information that is not legally protected. This includes information that does not unduly interfere with the mission of the department, infringe upon the rights of any person or compromise the legitimate safety and privacy interest of department employees, victims, witnesses or others. News Media Representatives - Those individuals who are directly employed by agencies of the electronic broadcast or print media. Free-lance workers in this field are to be regarded as members of the general public unless otherwise authorized by the Fire Chief. Public Information Officer (PIO) - The Fire Department representative who serves as the central source of information for release by the department. The PIO responds to requests for information by the news media and members of the community. The PIO is directly responsible to the Fire Chief. Designated PIO – A Fire Department officer appointed by Fire Administration or an Incident Commander to interact with the news media in the absence of the PIO. If this assignment is made at an incident scene, the designee will be identified as the PIO in the Incident Command Structure.

PERSONNEL AUTHORIZED TO RELEASE INFORMATION Fire Scene Information “Routine” Fires The Incident Commander, the Public Information Officer (PIO) or a designated PIO, shall brief news media representatives present at a scene with basic information concerning the incident in accordance with this policy. Arson Fires and/or Fires Involving Serious Injury or Fatality Fire Investigators have the obligation to preserve the integrity of a fire scene, to gather evidence, and to complete other needed investigative activities. In order to ensure that information released is accurate, ONLY THE INCIDENT COMMANDER, CHIEF FIRE INVESTIGATOR, PIO, OR DESIGNATED PIO IS AUTHORIZED TO RELEASE INFORMATION regarding the incident to the news media at the emergency scene. NOTE: The Omaha Police Department PIO will handle the release of information related to deaths resulting from arson related fires, or when death occurs as the result of a fire that occurs during the commission of another crime. Accident Scene or Other Non-Fire Incident Involving Injury or fatality The Incident Commander, the Public Information Officer (PIO) or a designated PIO, shall brief news media representatives present at the scene with basic information concerning the incident in accordance with this policy. OFD Involved Vehicle Accidents, Personal Injury or Property Damage, and Job Related Serious Injury/Fatality to Firefighter The Fire Chief, Administrative Assistant Chief, On-duty Assistant Chief, PIO or designated PIO are the only personnel allowed to release information to the media. This includes details on any accident or pending investigation into an accident, serious injury or fatality to a Firefighter. Day-to-Day Fire Department Operations Information regarding Fire Department goals and objectives, present policies, decisions on assigning personnel, decisions on the staffing of shifts or individual companies, administrative changes, and other similar fire or city management issues will be released by the Fire Chief or his/her designee only. Any member of the Omaha Fire Department, aside from the Fire Chief, or Assistant Chief, who is contacted by the news media for interviews or information about department activities shall notify, through their chain-of-command, the PIO in the Prevention & Public Education Division
prior to granting an interview or release of information (This does not apply to Division personnel who have prearranged policies in place through Fire Administration). If this request occurs after hours, the On-duty Assistant Chief shall be contacted for permission to conduct the interview. The Incident Commander or designated PIO can handle the majority of the PIO duties in the field. When Fire Investigation Unit personnel are on the scene, they will complete a Media Report for the PIO. However, in situations where a fire investigator was not called, and in the Incident Commander’s opinion the incident may generate media attention, it is imperative that the PIO or their designee receive information concerning the incident. This is especially important on incidents that occur overnight or when personnel are going on their days off. The Incident Commander or his or her designee shall email a synopsis of the event and any special information related to the event to the “OmaF Significant Event Report” mailbox. Special Circumstances The Fire Chief, Administrative Assistant Chief, On-duty Assistant Chief, or the Public Information Officer may grant permission to give feature story interviews to news media representatives. News representatives requesting feature story interviews should be directed to the Public Information Officer for needed authorization. Should the On-duty Assistant Chief agree to the production of a feature story after hours, or on weekends, or during holidays, the Public Information Officer should be notified (of the particulars of the story) via e-mail or voice mail. The Incident Commander or designee should also advise the Public Information Office of any Fire Department related incidents that will generate news media inquiries so that steps can be taken to prepare for future media questions. PUBLIC INFORMATION OFFICER (PIO) DUTIES Incident Scenes In order to assist the Incident Commander with the news media, the PIO can be called to the scene of any:    Serious emergencies as designated by the Incident Commander or On-duty Assistant Chief.    Long-term high profile incidents, as needed, regardless of the level of response per the Incident Commander.    Serious injury to a firefighter.    Fire Investigator-involved shooting.    Emergency incidents where information, such as evacuations, etc. and other public safety concerns, must be conveyed immediately to the public via all media agencies The PIO shall be available for on-call responses to any location during normal business hours. After hour call back of the PIO is at the discretion of the Fire Chief, Assistant Fire Chief or as per the multiple alarm call back policy. When minor fire incidents occur during the hours the Public Information Office is not staffed, refer news media representatives to the On-duty Assistant Chief from 0630 to 2230 hours only. Other Fire Department Business The PIO prepares and distributes Fire Department news releases as necessary. The PIO in most instances will arrange and assist at all news conferences. The PIO can assist in crisis situations within the department as assigned by the Fire Chief. The PIO can coordinate the release of information with other public service agencies when the department is working in a mutual effort. On-Scene Interviews Prior to conducting an on-camera interview, the PIO or designee will prepare the media off- camera to make certain that the media clearly understands the incident. The PIO or designee should be aware that some reporters may be carrying the interview live via their cellular phone or other device and should ask reporters if they are carrying the interview live so they are able to prepare accordingly. NOTE: Preparing the media off-camera will allow the media to ask questions prior to the interview and allow the PIO or designee to better formulate an appropriate response.

NOTE: Statements indicating possible liability, such as “the toaster malfunctioned” or “the owner wired it improperly”, should be avoided. Other Incidents General incident information through the Public Information Office, Incident Commander, or on- duty Assistant Chief, as per this policy. The Homicide Unit of the Omaha Police Department will release information on deaths due to arson or homicide. The Public Information Office and/or the Chief Fire Investigator (or his/her designee) is/are responsible for the following information releases:    Release of names of fatalities (The Omaha Police Department Public Information Officer handles fire fatalities’ name releases, as the result of homicide related to the crime of arson).    Information on suspects not arrested.    Biographical information of all arrests who have reached their 16 th birthday, condition of bail, and any information concerning future court appearances by the defendant.    Name, age, address, and description of persons for whom warrants have been issued.    Physical description(s) of any suspect(s) at large.

Fire Department Business    Internal communications and reports (i.e., day book and any supplementary reports to the original public report).    Fire employee personal and family information, and phone numbers. General Information Media Accountability The on-scene command officer or PIO will be responsible for designating a media staging location on-scene that provides the media with a safe location to film and report on the incident as appropriate. News representatives are not to be considered as the general public in the area of an incident or crime scene, but as persons who must fulfill their assigned tasks. However, they will not be allowed to position themselves beyond the established area of the fire scene.
News media will not be permitted to enter buildings or homes while warrants are being executed and persons or property are being removed by fire investigators or police officers. In all cases, NEWS media representatives will be allowed to remain wherever the general public is allowed. Media representatives will identify themselves using Station identification. Media will be expected to adhere to all traffic/parking laws around the scene and will not block emergency vehicles from accessing the scene. NOTE: The media shall not stop on the interstate to gain access to an accident/scene. Video Taping/Photography Any citizen or news media representative has the right to photograph or videotape firefighters performing their official duties. Firefighters will not physically block or cover the lenses of cameras or videotaping equipment at incidents where Firefighters are performing their duties. No Firefighter shall attempt to physically confiscate a videotape or camera equipment from any news media representative. Fire Investigators needing a copy of the video tape or camera film shall write an inter-office communication to the Chief of Police requesting that a copy of the film or video tape be obtained from the individual/organization for legitimate investigative purposes. OFD personnel are not responsible for censoring the media or ensuring responsible reporting practices; however, OFD personnel are responsible for scene security and may work with OPD personnel to shield a scene or evidence from view of the public or media when appropriate. Persons in Custody - No Media Contact Persons involved in incidents who are in the custody of fire investigators shall not be allowed to discuss such incidents with news media representatives. Photographing Persons in Custody Individuals in custody shall not be posed for the news media to photograph. Department photographs of an arrested person, when taken according to booking procedure may be released provided it will not jeopardize an investigation. The media may take photographs of the suspect at the scene if the suspect is in plain view of the media, provided it will not jeopardize an investigation. OFD personnel do not need to take extraordinary measures to shield the suspect at the scene unless photographing the suspect will jeopardize an investigation. If the media is photographing the suspect at a scene in plain view and the suspect is a juvenile, OPD officers will notify the photographer that the suspect is a juvenile as a courtesy.
Trespass on Private Property If a crime scene is on private property and the owner or person in charge of that property denies members of the news media permission to enter onto the property, and in the presence of police personnel or fire investigators so states it to the news representative, police/fire investigators have an obligation to ensure that the denial is honored. Timing on Release of Information Regarding Warrants No member of the Omaha Fire Department shall provide information to members of the news media about the possible execution of a warrant prior to the time the warrant has been served and the designated person(s) or property have been taken from the residence, business or other described area.

Workers’ Compensation Program - CorVel’s Role CorVel is the third-party administrator for the City of Omaha’s Workers’ Compensation Program. When there is a workers’ compensation claim, the employee is assigned a case number. When the employee contacts the CorVel case specialist via email, he/she shall include the case number and his/her full name in the subject line. It is recommended that communication with CorVel is via email for his/her documentation (paper trail) purposes, but the employee may also call and speak with the case specialist. The case specialist will assist the employee with authorization and scheduling any follow up treatment. The case specialist will also assist the employee throughout the work compensation process. The employee and case specialist will work together to facilitate proper treatment. Fire Services Personnel Manager and Employees The Fire Services Personnel Manager (FSPM) shall assist the employee with IOD policy, procedures, and the Limited Duty Program (if deemed appropriate). All medical paperwork related to the employee’s ability to perform job duties must be immediately forwarded to the FSPM. It is the employee’s responsibility to keep the Fire Services Personnel Manager routinely updated on follow-up appointments and changes in medical condition. For example, if the employee has an MRI, the employee shall inform the FSPM in a timely manner of the results as they impact the ability to perform the job. If a physician releases the employee to return to full duty, or conversely, if a physician orders limited/restricted duty, the employee must immediately (the same day) inform the FSPM. This will ensure continuous communication between the employee, CorVel, and Fire Management. Any questions regarding the limited duty program can be referred to the Fire Services Personnel Manager 402 444 6813 or the SWD BC at 402 444 5727. Improperly or Unpaid Workman’s Compensation Claim It is the practice of some physician’s offices to bill both the employee’s health insurance and workers’ compensation vendor. When claim payment from workers’ compensation is made, it is then credited to the employee’s health insurance. To keep insurance costs down, the employee should be made aware of this practice. It is recommended the employee should call his/her insurance billing vendor and let them know of any appointments associated with the Workers’ Comp claim and inform them not to pay since it is associated with workers’ compensation. The employee can follow up with the Bill Review Specialist at CorVel @ 402-393-0400 and then fax 402-393-0500 or scan the bill to that individual. The employee shall still inform the physician’s office that it is a Workers’ Compensation Claim and not give the health insurance information if possible, to keep administrative costs down for redundant claim’s processing. If the employee is receiving workers’ compensation bills, the employee shall inform the FSPM for follow up with CorVel. If the bills are ignored they may go to collections.
Scheduling of Medical Appointments for IOD Related Injuries Division Personnel The employee’s follow up medical appointments for IOD (including physical therapy, work hardening, and work transition for follow up care of IOD injuries) shall be scheduled during regular work hours whenever possible. The employee shall attempt to schedule appointments at the beginning or end of their daily work schedule before 0900 hours or after 1500 hours. If unable to do so, the employee will be expected to return to work from all scheduled appointments prior to 1530 hours. Special circumstances must be approved by the BC of SWD. During scheduled work hours, the employee’s drive time to and from IOD related appointments is considered IOD time and shall be logged as such in Workforce. If the employee does not return to work after an IOD-related appointment, the IOD time will end at the conclusion of the appointment. Medical appointments outside regular work hours are not considered work hours for overtime purposes, nor are they considered IOD hours. Suppression Personnel The employee’s medical appointments, including PT/work hardening/work transition for follow up care of IOD injuries, shall not be scheduled during regular work hours, except as detailed below. The hours are not considered compensable for OT and/or IOD (exception see Limited Duty for IOD Injury/Illness). When an employee suffers a potential IOD injury and until such time as the employee has received a diagnosis of their condition (when a diagnosis has been made, the preceding paragraph shall apply), the employee shall make every reasonable effort to schedule their doctor's appointments outside of their regular work hours However, if doing so results in an unreasonable delay of the appointment, the employee will be permitted to have their appointments during regular work hours until they have received a diagnosis of their condition. Employees will be expected to return to work, if permitted by the doctor, expeditiously after such appointments. If such appointment is scheduled during work hours, the employee's drive time to and from the appointment is considered IOD time and shall be logged as such in workforce. If the doctor imposes restrictions on the employee resulting in the employee not being permitted to work, the employee will remain on IOD until IOD is either approved or denied as contemplated by the Collective Bargaining Agreement. If an employee is not going to return to work, they will immediately contact their chain of command as well as the Fire Services Personnel Manager to make them aware of the situation. Cancellation of IOD Appointments IOD appointments should only be canceled or rescheduled for justifiable circumstances. If an employee fails to keep any scheduled appointments, such action may be deemed noncompliant with the prescribed medical treatment, and workers’ compensation benefits could potentially be
terminated. Canceling or rescheduling appointments must have prior approval of the Fire Services Personnel Manager and/or the SWD BC. The employee must also contact the assigned case specialist and the medical provider as soon as reasonably possible. IOD Mileage Reimbursement Mileage for transportation to and from IOD appointments are reimbursable. Contact the Fire Service Personnel Manager to obtain the form. IOD mileage is NOT managed on Workforce. PROCEDURES FOR THE LIMITED-DUTY PROGRAM The Limited Duty Program, if appropriate, becomes available once an attending physician agrees or recommends that an employee with an injury or illness is able to perform limited duties. A physician’s note is required and must identify current restrictions, including an estimated time- frame for recovery, prior to working a limited duty assignment. The employee must notify the Fire Services Personnel Manager or SWD BC to obtain information on when to report for limited duty. This determination is based on the date, shift and hours already worked Limited duty begins with an orientation from the SWD Fire Services Personnel Manager or SWD BC. The orientation will provide the employee with guidelines, policies, expectations, and job assignment. When an employee accepts a limited duty assignment, the employee shall work within his/her medical restrictions. It is the employee’s responsibility to remain within those restrictions and to inform his/her supervisor of such restrictions. If the employee feels they are breaching those restrictions, the employee shall immediately contact the FSPM. If the FSPM is unavailable, the employee shall contact the SWD BC. It is the employee’s responsibility to adhere to their medical restrictions in all activities, at all times, both on and off duty. Limited duty personnel receive a thirty (30) minute lunch break and two (2) fifteen minute breaks during their work day. The employee shall be permitted to combine his/her lunch break and two (2) fifteen minute breaks for a one hour lunch break. Only under special circumstances, and at the supervisor’s sole discretion, will an employee on limited duty be allowed to work through his/her lunch and breaks in order to leave one hour earlier. Limited duty is intended to be temporary to allow an employee to recover from an illness or injury. Limited duty assignments shall not generally exceed one year from date of such accident, injury, or illness. The one year period shall not be for a combined period that exceeds 365 consecutive days of all leave types utilized during the year. If an employee reaches maximum medical improvement, the employee shall be evaluated to determine if he/she can return to full duty. If he/she cannot, the City will follow the City Policy relative to accommodations. If no accommodation can be made, the employee may have the opportunity to pursue a disability pension.
IOD and NON-IOD Injuries According to the Union Contract (Article 41, Section 3), it is understood by and between the parties that such assignment may be any work the employee is qualified to perform, notwithstanding that such work may not be considered traditional firefighter work and may not fall within employee’s job description. Such work assignment will be a forty hour work week (with either five (5), eight-hour days or four (4), ten-hour days and may be in any City Department. If the employee is assigned to limited duty within the Fire Department, such employee shall work the hours normally worked by the Division to which he/she is assigned. If an employee is assigned to other City Departments or Division duties, the employee may be assigned to eight-hour days. Limited Duty for IOD Injury/Illness If an IOD employee declines a limited duty assignment, the following paragraph shall govern the rules of IOD and work schedule. If an IOD employee has been offered a limited duty assignment and that employee has declined the L/D Assignment, the employee shall not be moved to a 40-hour schedule. The employee will remain on the suppression work schedule and use the appropriate sick leave/annual leave bank balances. When the employee is using sick/annual leave but has scheduled appointments during their regular duty shift, the time at the appointments will be considered compensatory IOD hours and shall reflect that on Workforce. In this case, travel time is not considered IOD, but mileage reimbursement can be claimed. Limited Duty for NON-IOD Injury/Illness Limited duty approval shall be limited to no more than eight (8) employees injured off duty. The employee shall request a limited duty assignment by submitting an OFD 25 to the Fire Chief though his/her chain of command. The employee shall also contact and request a limited duty assignment from the SWD BC. The employee is required to use 240 hours of sick leave before entering the Limited Duty Program. After this and the employee has met the other conditions, employees will be assigned in order of application. The employee remains on the Suppression books until given a limited duty assignment. Once limited duty begins the employee’s schedule is changed from a 56-hour work week to a 40-hour work week. The SWD BC and Fire Services Personnel Manager will then be responsible for assuring all leave requests and Workforce entries are properly entered. NON-IOD Doctor or PT Appointments for Employees on Limited Duty For employees with an off-duty injury or illness, using sick leave, who want to minimize their sick leave usage hours, it is acceptable to utilize break and lunch time (60 minutes) for doctor’s appointments or physical therapy appointments. Restrictions and Outside Employment Employee shall not accept employment outside City service or participate actively in the management or operation of any business or enterprise that would prolong recovery while on IOD, or sick leave, or while on limited duty assignment. (Reference SOP PERS 3-0 Code of Conduct) If an employee is working in his/her City classification with medical restrictions, any approved outside employment or other activities must not exceed the limitations placed by health care professionals. Violation of this policy may result in termination of benefits under the Injured on duty/Workers’ Compensation provisions, and/or disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. Restricted Hours If employees are working less than full time (4/10 hours or 5/8 hours per day) due to a physician’s note prescribing reduced work hours, all appointments will be required to be scheduled outside the reduced work hours. Limited Duty personnel are not required to work on City holidays or weekends. Limited duty personnel are not permitted to work trade time or call back. Refer to current CBA Article 37, section 5 for previously agreed upon trade time. Once the complaint form is completed, the citizen shall contact the Professional Standards Office. The Professional Standards Investigator will document information in regards to the complaint on an OFD 9a, Notification Complaint Form, and schedule an appointment to interview the citizen. The Fire Chief or appointed designee will be notified by the Professional Standards Investigator of a pending investigation. To Register a Complaint A citizen wishing to make a complaint will obtain and fill out the complaint form, OFD 9. The complaint form can be obtained from the following locations:     Omaha Fire stations     Mayor’s office     Omaha Public libraries Once the form is complete, the citizen shall contact the Professional Standards Office to schedule an interview. Should it be impossible for the citizen to be able to write out the complaint the Professional Standards Investigator will assist the citizen by filling out the OFD 9 and note on the form the reason why the citizen was unable to write their complaint. Complaints that Do Not Result in a Complete Investigation The Professional Standards Investigator may handle a complaint or general inquiry without conducting a formal investigation. This would be a complaint that obviously has no foundation, is a general inquiry, or a complaint that does not formalize. No Foundation — a complaint that is filed that has no foundation has the following criteria. 1. The complaint filed is out of the jurisdiction of the Omaha Fire Department. 2. The complaint filed shows that no such employee or incident occurred to warrant an investigation. 3. The complaint filed shows the allegation, if true, does not rise to a conduct violation. An investigation will be initiated but once it is discovered that there is no foundation, this will be documented on an inter-office communication and brought to the Fire Chief’s or appointed designee’s attention of why the allegation has no foundation.
General Inquiry — refers to cases immediately solved to the satisfaction of the citizen without requiring a more extensive investigation. Inquiries will be documented on an OFD 9c, Contact Information, and forwarded to the Fire Chief’s or appointed designee’s office. Then filed for any relevant future reference. Did Not Formalize (DNF) — the complaint process was initiated but the citizen did not come forward to formalize the complaint with the Professional Standards Office. This includes incidents that occur and the citizen wants to bring it to the attention of the department but does not want to come forward to file a formal complaint. This will be documented on an inter-office communication as Did Not Formalize, and forwarded to the Fire Chief’s or appointed designee’s office. Then filed for any relevant future reference. The “Citizen’s Complaints” referred to in this policy deals with the firefighter’s or fire officer’s conduct as governed by the Fire Department Standard Operating Procedure, all Fire Department General Orders, Bulletins, Rules and Regulations, Code of Conduct, Firefighter Code of Ethics and the Collective Bargaining Agreement with the Fire Union. Alleged criminal acts by firefighters or fire officers will be referred to the Police Department for proper investigation. GENERAL GUIDELINES General Use Guidelines of Omaha policies including City of Omaha Human Resource Policy # 38 Social Media. On-duty personnel, if part of their duties and responsibilities, may use Social Media specifically for community engagement and outreach activities. General Prohibitions/Cautions OFD personnel may not divulge private or confidential information gained solely and exclusively by reason of their employment, nor make or publish any statements, endorsements or provide materials that could reasonably be considered to represent the views or positions of the OFD without express authorization of the Fire Chief or designee. Personnel are prohibited from releasing confidential and or proprietary, patient, or citizen information. Medical information and records are confidential and should not be disclosed to anyone, at any time, even if the disclosure is to describe the OFD or any of its members in a positive light. This applies whether the individual is posting to a personal site, a Department site or commenting on other Social Media sites. The use of OFD computers or technology by non-personnel is prohibited. OFD personnel should expect that any information created, transmitted, downloaded, exchanged, or discussed in a public online forum while the employee is on duty may be accessed by the City of Omaha, the OFD and/or DOTComm at any time without prior notice. OFD personnel should be aware that, in addition to discipline, they may expose themselves up to civil liability if they do any of the following: 1. Publish or post false information that harms the reputation of another person, group, or organization (defamation). 2. Publish or post private facts and personal information about someone without their permission that has not been previously revealed to the public, is not of legitimate public concern, and would be offensive to a reasonable person. 3. Use someone else’s name, likeness, or other personal attributes without that person’s permission for an exploitative purpose. 4. Publish the creative work of another, trademarks, or certain confidential business information without the permission of the owner.

OFD personnel should be aware that privacy settings and Social Media sites are constantly in flux, and they should never assume that personal information posted on such sites is protected. Professional Conduct When engaging in Social Media or social networking activities, all personnel will maintain a level of professionalism both on-duty and off-duty that is consistent with the mission, code of conduct, and code of ethics of the OFD as referenced in SOP PERS 3-0 Code of Conduct and Firefighter Code of Ethics. Personnel must be aware of their role in representing the OFD on Social Media outlets and should proceed with care in how they are presenting themselves to colleagues, affiliates, competitors, and the general public. Personnel should be sensitive to the fact that any comments on a website or Social Media outlet are public content and immediately searchable and accessible by others as well as subject to being republished on other Social Media platforms. Personnel should not send or display any information that may be construed as offensive or harassing while on duty or while using City equipment.    Offensive or harassing messages include, but are not limited to: pornographic images; sexual references; comments regarding an individual’s gender, age, sexual orientation, religious beliefs, national origin, disability, or any characteristic protected by law; racial slurs; or comments that intimidate or threaten another person. Personnel must respect copyright, fair use, and financial disclosure laws and link back to original sources whenever possible. Off-Duty Social Media Use While off-duty, OFD personnel are free to express themselves as private citizens on Social Media sites within the parameters established; however, such expressions which impair working relationships or activities of the OFD and/or the City of Omaha or bring an undue burden upon the effective performance of Department or City functions will not be tolerated. Such expressions will not violate any confidentiality of their position or any investigation, impede the performance of any employee’s duties, impair the discipline and harmony among coworkers, or negatively affect the public perception of the OFD and/or the City of Omaha. As public employees, OFD personnel are cautioned that on- or off-duty speech, made pursuant to their official duties, (that is, speech owing its existence to the employee’s professional duties and responsivities), MAY NOT be protected speech under the First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution.
1. Such speech may form the basis for discipline, up to and including termination, if it is deemed detrimental to the mission of the OFD and/or the City of Omaha. 2. OFD personnel should assume that their posted speech and related activity on Social Media sites will reflect upon their official office, the City of Omaha and this Department and should conduct themselves accordingly. No information, audio, videos or pictures gathered while on Department business (including but not limited to emergency calls, meetings, drills, details, trainings, or anything obtained on organization property or at organization functions) may be shared or posted in any format without the approval and written consent of the Fire Chief or designee. For safety and security reasons, personnel are cautioned not to disclose on Social Media their employment with the OFD including displaying OFD logos, uniforms, or similar identifying items.    Note the OFD badge is copyrighted and shall not be used or reproduced in any manner without written permission of the Fire Chief or designee. Digital Media Personnel are not permitted to capture digital media content including photographs, audio, or videos via recording devices while on-duty, specifically including during an emergency response. Personnel are only permitted to acquire digital media content while on-duty with prior written authorization from the Fire Chief or their designee. ADMINISTRATION 6-0 PERSONAL GROOMING/HYGIENE REVISION HISTORY REVISION #   REVISION DESCRIPTION          DATE REVISED        AUTHOR         REVIEWED BY         APPROVED BY 1             Periodic Revision          Nov 2015         Hunter 723        McEvoy 680          Kanger 601 PURPOSE AND POLICY Members of the Omaha Fire Department are in frequent contact with citizens from a variety of backgrounds. The Fire Department is judged through each individual contact made with the public by its members. As a result, it is important that each member give careful attention to their own personal actions and appearance. Your conduct, both on and off duty, should be in good taste and acceptable to the public. A member's appearance and conduct will often determine the initial impression made on citizens they deal with. It should be remembered that to the public, each member is the Fire Department; therefore, pride in a member's appearance reflects upon the image of the entire Fire Department. A good impression upon the public, however, involves more than a neat appearing uniform, and is dependent upon all of the following to form the complete picture. SPEECH - ACTIONS The fire officer and firefighter, by their speech, actions, tone of voice or gestures, will reflect their attitude toward people and the profession, and will be judged accordingly by the public. When you approach a citizen with a scowl and threatening attitude, you will usually antagonize them, and their response will be resentment and a reason to lodge a complaint against you. A friendly, cheerful approach to the public is an extremely effective tool to gain cooperation. Though an officer should not use an apologetic manner, he/she should, however, be quick and willing to offer an apology when an apology is due. Being courteous to everyone, without exception, and yet firm in the enforcement of the law, makes you a good fire officer. Efficiency, courtesy and composure will make a good impression on all concerned. Often, in a moment of stress and tragedy, people will become distracted and excited. At these times, it is especially important that the fire officer acts as a true professional and takes charge of the situation with a calm assurance. There is strength in calmness and your bearing will be a sign of confidence for all persons around.

PERSONAL CARE – HYGIENE Personal grooming is important to everyone, but it holds special significance for the firefighters who are in frequent contact with the public. Inappropriate haircuts, mustaches and uniform dress must be avoided. This is necessary to assure the life safety of members by making certain that there be no impediment when members use face pieces from masks and other emergency equipment and to assure that employees present a positive image at all times. Although fire fighters get involved in all forms of tasks and duties that often get them extremely dirty, such as "working fires" or rescues from excavations, flour mills, etc., for the most part the public expects them to be always neat and clean. Members on duty shall at all times wear the proper uniform unless otherwise instructed by their supervisor. All officers are responsible for the condition and appearance of themselves and their personnel and they shall pay special attention to the following items: Male Personnel Head Hair The Fire Department does not prescribe a specified hairstyle as long as hair is maintained within these guidelines. Hair shall be neat, clean, well-trimmed and properly combed at all times, except under the most adverse conditions, such as emergency operations. Hair on top of head will not be overly long and when combed, will not extend past the top of eyebrows, or protrude from under the band of the headgear at the forehead. The sides of the hair must not extend beyond midpoint of the ears. Hair on the back of the head must be kept trimmed and cannot extend beyond the bottom of the collar or touch the top edge of the tee shirt or sweatshirt when person is standing erect with eyes to the horizontal and not wearing a tie. In addition to the above, the depth of the hair mass, when measured at right angles from any part of the scalp (outward or upward), shall not exceed two (2) inches. In no case will the bulk or length of the hair interfere with the proper fitting of any Fire Department headgear. Hair will not be worn in extreme or fad style such as Mohawk, braids, ponytails, ducktails. Faddish or designer razor cuts and must not be dyed an unnatural color or be an unnatural extreme contrast. Men's hair will not contain nor have any visible foreign items attached to it. Braids/cornrows for male personnel are permitted provided that they are braided as close to the scalp as possible. Individual free dangling braids are not permitted. Braid styles shall be worn braided from front to back in straight, neatly styled rows. Any other style not conforming to this requirement will not be permitted.
Wigs Wigs, hairpieces and similar devices shall not be worn to cover hairstyles or lengths not conforming to this code. However, hairpieces and/or wigs conforming to this standard may be worn for reasons of baldness and/or physical disfigurement. Items used to hold hair in place will be concealed as much as possible. Side Burns Sideburns shall not extend downward below the middle of ear. They must be evenly tapered and end in a clean shaven horizontal line. Sideburns shall be kept neatly trimmed and will not be bushy or flared nor any wider than one (l) inch width at any point and shall be no thicker than 1/2 inch from temple or cheek. Mustache Mustaches must be neatly trimmed, tapered and will leave the upper lip visible. Mustaches will not extend more than 1/2 inch downward beyond the corner of the mouth and shall not extend more than 3/4-inch horizontal from the corner of the mouth. No handlebar or Fu Manchu types of mustaches will be allowed. Goatee or Beards Beards or goatees will not be allowed except for facial hair immediately below the lip and not exceeding one-half inch in length or width. This facial hair shall be neatly trimmed. Other than the above, the face must be clean shaven. In no event will any head hair, sideburns, mustaches or other facial hair be allowed if it impedes the proper usage of face pieces, masks or other emergency equipment. Lip Beard Lip beards will be kept neatly trimmed and close to the face to avoid a possibility of a defective seal when wearing the face-piece. Lip beards will not exceed ½” wide x ½” long. Pseudofolliculitis Barbe At the present time, the wearing of beards is only permitted to members of the Department who have a legitimate medical condition (such as Pseudofolliculitis Barbe).    Personnel must obtain a note from their personal physicians and from a City approved physician that states that they suffer from a qualifying medical condition.    Personnel must then shave with hair clippers, utilizing a No. 2 barber’s blade on the clippers, to get as close a shave as possible without aggravating the medical condition.    Whatever shaving technique may be authorized, personnel must still pass their respiratory device fit tests.
Female Personnel Head Hair Hair shall be neat, clean and properly combed at all times, except under the most adverse conditions, such as emergency operations. Front of hair (bangs) will not extend beyond eyebrows or protrude from under the band of the headgear at the forehead. Hair on sides of head must not extend beyond bottom of ear lobe. Any hair not conforming to this requirement must be secured (mechanically restrained) to meet these requirements. Hair on back of head, that is not secured, may touch but not extend beyond the bottom of the shirt collar when person is standing erect with eyes to the horizontal and not wearing a tie. In addition to the above, the thickness of the hair mass, once compressed, when measured at right angles from any part of the scalp (outward and upward), shall not exceed two (2) inches. In no case will the hair length, bulk or hairstyle interfere with the proper fitting of any Fire Department headgear. Hair will not be worn in extreme, fad or design styles. Decorative items such as ribbons or bows will not be worn in the hair. The device used must not interfere with safe and proper use of helmets, face pieces or other required equipment. However, hairpieces and/or wigs conforming to this standard may be worn for reasons of baldness and/or physical disfigurement. Lipstick colors shall be conservative.

Female personnel may wear nail polish providing colors are conservative. Male personnel may wear clear nail polish. Decorative or studded nails are not allowed. Jewelry The following items of jewelry may be worn by all personnel:    Wrist watch/Bracelets - maximum of one watch and one bracelet or two bracelets may be worn, which are not overly large and do not interfere with quick donning of gloves. An engagement / wedding ring for the purpose of this guideline is considered one ring.    Religious medallions or identification chains (ex: medi-alert) - these items must be concealed by uniform shirt. No other neck ornaments or jewelry are permitted. All personnel should be aware of the safety hazards these jewelry items present and use caution when wearing them. Tattoos Tattoos shall be in good taste and shall not be in violation of the City of Omaha’s Anti- Discrimination policy. Any visible tattoo that is determined to be in violation of this policy or that is offensive in nature, e.g., obscene sayings or nude figures, must be covered while on duty. The Fire Chief has the final determination if a tattoo is offensive or in violation of the City of Omaha’s Anti-Discrimination policy. Teeth Clean And In Good Condition Keeping one's teeth clean and in good condition is as necessary for proper grooming and health as any other item of personal maintenance.
Clean Hands Even the dirtiest hands can be easily cleaned up by the use of any number of commercial hand cleaners used by most mechanics. Using this type hand cleaner under the fingernails will quickly remove grease, which may normally be difficult to remove. Body and Breath Free of Odor By regular use of a good deodorant and mouth wash, these will not be a problem, even during the times of excitement and/or exertion which is common in the firefighting profession. It is intended to honor both active and retired firefighters. SCOPE These procedures and guidelines will assist designed Department Officers in coordinating a Fire Department funeral. These guidelines are also intended to provide necessary procedural information to attending firefighters. FUNERAL OFFICER POSITIONS The full responsibilities of the individuals and groups listed are described in more detail under the Job Responsibilities section of this SOP. Survivor Action Officer (Fire/Assistant Chief) This is the person, often the Chief or designee, who assigns and oversees all activities necessary to assist the next of kin and family, manages the Fire Department's involvement in the funeral, and takes care of administrative matters relating to the firefighter's death. He will also give instructions when appropriate to dispatch the death announcement. Family Liaison Officer (Battalion Chief) A Family Liaison Officer, usually the appropriate Battalion Chief, should be identified to act as a link between the family and the Department. This person, who should be on call 24 hours, provides assistance to the family during the funeral process and relays information to the Survivor Action Officer, according to the family's wishes. Funeral Officer The Funeral Officer (usually a close friend on the Department) is responsible for implementing the wishes of the family as expressed through the Family Liaison Officer in regard to the Fire Department's involvement in the funeral itself. This position is assigned by the Survivor Action Officer at the beginning of the funeral planning process. The Funeral Officer shall be responsible for coordination of the funeral, procession, and reception, among other assigned duties.

Chaplain The amount of involvement the Chaplain has will depend upon the family's wishes and/or religion. One option that can be proposed is a shared responsibility between the clergy of the family's choice and the Department Chaplain. Here again, the family's wishes prevail. Areas handled by the Chaplain will be:    Initial notification of next of kin, with the Chief or his designee    Comfort and counseling of surviving family members    Prayer service in the home or mortuary    Church services    Cemetery interment    Follow-up counseling for the surviving family members NOTIFICATION The notification of the death and all available information shall be made to members of the department by Personnel Order. It will be the responsibility of the on-duty station Captains to contact off-duty station personnel and relay funeral information. FUNERAL TYPES There are five circumstances under which a death occurs and the Department takes action. Type 1 An active member is killed in the line of duty, either during an incident or as a direct result of an incident. A Survivor Action Officer, Family Liaison Officer, Funeral Officer and Chaplain will be assigned to take charge of the funeral detail. All notifications are made (family, Department personnel, Chaplain, etc.). At the request of the Fire Chief or designee, a Chaplain is designated to offer solace to the family. Off duty and selected on duty personnel attend the funeral ceremony. For funerals that are conducted within the City, a static display of apparatus in front of the fire stations with the members in Class A uniform is appropriate along the processional route. The commanding officer at that fire station will order a hand salute as the hearse passes. Approval for the number of companies to be placed out of service shall be made by the Survivor Action Officer in charge of the detail. The flag that was flying at the member's last place of assignment is retired from duty and, along with a memorial resolution from the Office of the Mayor, is presented to the member's appropriate survivor by the Fire Chief or designee.
The member's badge is retired from service and, after being properly mounted, is presented to the member's appropriate survivor by the Fire Chief or designee. Type 2 An active member dies during the course of employment and the member's death is attributable to a line-of-duty cause, but is not directly incident related. (Any of the presumptive causes or an accidental cause while on duty.) A Survivor Action Officer, Family Liaison Officer, Funeral Officer and Chaplain will be assigned to take charge of the funeral detail. Notifications are made. The Chaplain is designated, at the request of the Fire Chief or designee, to offer solace to the family. Attendance and funeral detail is the same as Type No. 1. Type 3 A member dies during the course of employment with the death being judged as “not in the line of duty”. Fire Department personnel and Chaplain notifications are made and, if the member's survivors so request, Survivor Action Officer, Family Liaison Officer, Funeral Officer and Chaplain will be assigned. At the request of the Fire Chief or designee, a Chaplain is designated to offer solace to the family. Type 4 A retired member dies. Fire Department personnel and Chaplain notifications are made and, if the member's survivors so request, a Survivor Action Officer, Family Liaison Officer, Funeral Officer and Chaplain will be assigned. At the request of the Fire Chief or designee, a Chaplain is designated to offer solace to the family. Type 5 A past member of the Fire Department or a dignitary closely associated with the Department dies and the Department wishes to bestow funeral honors. At the request of the Fire Chief or designee, a Chaplain is designated to offer solace to the family. A Survivor Action Officer, Family Liaison Officer, Funeral Officer and Chaplain may be assigned. Honorary Pallbearers may be assigned according to the family's wishes. Use of apparatus and uniform detail will normally be limited to funerals that are conducted within the boundaries of the City of Omaha. With approval of the Fire Chief, Department apparatus may be assigned to funerals conducted outside the City.
FUNERAL OPTIONS In the event that an active member of the Fire Department dies, the Fire Chief or designee will call the family or responsible party and give notification of deaths and particular details of the incident. At this time, the Fire Department will offer its services to the family in making funeral arrangements. The family of the deceased will make the decision about how simple or elaborate the funeral will be. The department (including Chaplain) may offer certain options for the family to consider, but ultimately it is the family who makes the final decisions. If the family accepts the offered service, the Fire Department shall encourage all firefighters assigned to the battalion in which the deceased member worked to attend the funeral, unless the member was killed in the line of duty, in which case all off duty and selected on duty personnel shall be urged to attend the funeral. Captains will be held responsible for the contacting of their respective company members. Honor Guard If an honor guard is requested by the family, it becomes the responsibility of the Funeral Director and the Department to assure that the request is honored. Ideally, a list of honor guards or members comprising an honor guard will be available. Typically, the honor guards are to report in their Class A uniforms, with uniform caps on. If two honor guards are used (Fire Department and military honor guards) they are to be placed at the head and foot of the casket. If a Fire Department honor guard only is used, two members with Fire Department flag and the U.S. flag with stands will be displayed. In all cases, the U.S. flag is displayed at the head of the casket. Refer to the United States Flag Code for full details on how the display the flag. Pallbearers in Uniform If the family chooses to use Fire Department personnel as pallbearers, it must be determined which firefighters the family would like to have in the service. Usually six to eight are needed. They wear Class A uniforms, with uniform caps on. If the body is to be moved from the mortuary to the church, pallbearers should arrive early enough to assist, if necessary. At the church, the pallbearers will form outside the church and be ready to bring in the casket. They will remain covered when bringing in the casket, uncover when seated, and remain so until after the services. After the services, they will cover before bringing out the casket. Transportation A department vehicle and driver may be offered to the immediate next of kin during the viewing and funeral period. This is entirely at the discretion of the Fire Chief.
Meals With the amount of arrangements that have to be dealt with and the emotional distress that accompanies a death, meals are often a matter of aggravation for the deceased's family. Traditionally, family and friends provide food, or unions or associations may be able to provide for these needs, including preparation, delivery and financial support. Should these arrangements be needed, the Survivor Action Officer should appoint someone to coordinate these efforts. The Funeral Officer is usually assigned this duty. Child Care If child care presents a problem for the family during the viewing and funeral period, this need should be identified and assistance provided. The fire union or its auxiliary may be of service in this area. Procession The family may indicate the desire for a procession from the funeral home and/or church to the cemetery. The procession process starts with the staging of vehicles at the funeral home prior to the funeral beginning and ends upon arrival at the cemetery. Specifics usually are under the direction of the Funeral Director with the cooperation of the Funeral Officer. Among the options to consider:    The use of department vehicle(s) - caisson, flower vehicle, miscellaneous transportation    Procession route - may include the deceased firefighter's fire station, home, or other special considerations    Providing static display of apparatus – if requested or appropriate along the procession route    Providing crossed ladders or aerial equipment - if requested or appropriate at the cemetery entrance Caisson A fire apparatus may be appropriate as a caisson to carry the deceased individual's turn out clothes and helmet. Should this option be exercised, the apparatus will have to be taken out of service for a period of time, cleaned, draped in bunting, and retrofitted or adapted to easily accept these options. Flower Vehicle A piece of fire apparatus can serve as a flower vehicle in the procession. It will have to be taken out of service, cleaned, draped in bunting, and retrofitted to carry flowers. Last Alarm Service A traditional bell ringing ceremony at the end of the church service may be exercised, signifying the firefighter's last alarm. A short reading accompanies the ringing of the bell.
Musical Arrangements Here again, the family selects the musical arrangements for the service and cemetery, to include choirs, bands, singers, organ arrangement, etc. These are coordinated through the Family Liaison Officer. Readings The family should decide the appropriate Scripture readings or verses and who will read them. Arrangements are made with the family church and clergy involved for both the church and cemetery services. Eulogy Typically, the family decides what, when, and where the eulogy will be presented. This may be appropriate at any one of the steps in the entire ceremony, at the funeral home, the church, or the cemetery. A clergy member, family member, or friend form the Department may perform this task. The Family Liaison Officer makes the appropriate contacts with the church and Funeral Director. Crossed Aerial Ladders Should the family wish to have crossed aerial ladders at the cemetery entrance, the Family Liaison Officer should forward this request to the Survivor Action Officer for coordination and approval. The implementation of this request is handled by the Funeral Officer. Static Display of Outside Equipment The family may choose to exercise a static display of Department apparatus with fire personnel at attention and saluting the passing casket during the procession. This final tribute may be set up anywhere; however, it is usually at the church, fire station on the procession route, or at the cemetery entrance. The Funeral Officer is responsible for handling the placement of all vehicles during the procession. Burial in Uniform The Department uniform can be used should the family wish to bury the deceased this way. The Family Liaison Officer will deliver the clothing to the Funeral Director upon request. Closed Casket If the family wishes a closed casket, a picture of the deceased in uniform and the deceased's clean helmet may be placed on top of the casket or displayed on a small table near the casket during the viewing. These can later be presented to the family. Walk Through A scheduled walk through paying tribute to the deceased may be exercised either at the church or the funeral home as a form of paying tribute from fellow firefighters. This should be
coordinated with the Funeral Director and the Funeral Officer. If this is done, the fire personnel line up single file by rank and agency. The formation then files through single file past the casket, stopping briefly to pay respects. The contingent then exits the chapel. Refreshments or Post Services Reception Should the family approve, an after service reception may be held at a church hall, school cafeteria, or union hall. The Funeral Officer should coordinate the event. Bunting Station and apparatus bunting should be clean and available for Department use with short notice. The front of all fire stations will be draped with black bunting from the time of notification that a Department member has passed away until thirty days* or fifteen days** after the funeral. Should fire trucks be used for caissons or flower vehicle, they too should be bunted at that time. Half Staff Flags American flags can be brought to half-staff from the time of notification that a Department member has passed away until thirty days* or fifteen days** after the funeral. When a flag is at half-staff, no other flags should be flown on the same halyard (See United States Flag Code) Badge Shrouding Shrouding of a badge is accomplished by placing a 1/2" to 3/4" piece of black material horizontally at the badge's midpoint entirely around the badge. The shroud should be placed on badges from the time of notification that a Department member has passed away and shall remain on the badge until thirty days* or fifteen days** after the funeral. * thirty days for a TYPE NO.1 funeral (Refer to Funeral Types) ** fifteen days for TYPE NO.2 and TYPE NO.3 funerals (Refer to Funeral Types) JOB RESPONSIBILITIES Survivor Action Officer It is recommended that the Fire Chief assume or delegate the position of the Survivor Action Officer to oversee liaison to the next of kin and all funeral-related activities. Typically, the Survivor Action Officer is a special staff assignment. As a direct representative of the Fire Chief, the Survivor Action Officer should receive the full cooperation of the entire Department. The Survivor Action Officer coordinates the activities of a number of personnel assigned to handle specific aspects of the funeral arrangements and to assist the surviving family. Their principal concern is the ongoing welfare of the next of kin. It is their responsibility to ensure whatever assistance is necessary is provided to assist the family through the crisis, conduct the funeral, and settle the affairs of the deceased member.
Additional duties of the Survivor Action Officer are as follows:    Confirm that the next of kin has been notified    Contact City Benefits Manager in the event of a death of an active duty Fire Department employee    Determine which Fire Department vehicles will be used as caissons or flower vehicles    Notify all department personnel of the death    Confirm the notification of all outside agencies and vacationing personnel    Arrange to have flags lowered to half-staff and bunting placed on stations as appropriate    Follow up contacts when funeral arrangements have been determined    Conduct a coordination meeting with the "task force" of officers as soon as possible (Family Liaison and Funeral Officer) so that all key individuals will be instructed to make the appropriate contacts and when the next meeting will be scheduled  Be a key contact person for outside agencies, news media and other departments in relation to the death and subsequent ceremony. The Family Liaison Officer provides the logistical support to the family throughout the funeral process and should have a Department vehicle assigned to him for the entire time. The duties are as follows:    Discuss all aspects of the funeral process and its ceremonies with the family, Funeral Officer, and funeral director. The Family Liaison Officer must be able to relay
information to the Department as to what level of involvement the Department will have in the funeral process in accordance with the family's wishes.    Inform the family as to the various traditional fire service funeral options that can be included in a service. This is accomplished with the cooperation of the Funeral Officer and funeral director. These might include readings, music, honor guards and military formations. All of the requests made by the surviving family must be relayed to the Survivor Action Officer for delegation to the "task force" of officers. The Family Liaison Officer may assist the family in determining the following:    Type of interment    Which funeral home will be used    Which church/large assembly area will be used    Which clergy will be used, including the Department Chaplain    Which cemetery will be used    If the deceased will be buried in uniform    Number of primary pallbearers and whether honorary pallbearers will be used    The length of church service, as well as:  Readers of the Scripture  What Scripture will be read  Music at the church  Who will deliver the sermon or eulogy  Ending last alarm bell service    The length of the wake (if any) and a tentative schedule    The ceremonies that will take place at the cemetery:  Band  Firing Squad  Readings and who will perform them  Eulogy and who will deliver it  Taps    Procession Items:  If a fire apparatus be used as a caisson or if a conventional hearse will be used (In some Departments the fire apparatus from the deceased member's most recent duty station is taken out of service and used as the hearse, with minor modifications made to accommodate the casket.)  If a fire apparatus will be used as a flower vehicle  Whether personnel will walk alongside the caisson or drive in the procession The Family Liaison Officer should also:    Obtain all articles of clothing that the deceased will wear (except shoes) and deliver them to the funeral director/officer
   Identify and determine any other special considerations on behalf of the family and the special requests per the individual's personal information sheet    Maintain 24-hour contact with the family for their assistance and the same contact with the Survivor Action Officer    Obtain a recent photograph of the deceased for the funeral director Funeral Officer The Funeral Officer's duties include the primary responsibility of insuring that the wishes of the family and the special requests of the individual are coordinated with the Fire Department, Funeral Director, and Chaplains. He provides coordination and interaction with the Funeral Director and the church and the cemetery, and arranges and directs the funeral procession. The Funeral Officer reports directly to the Survivor Action Officer and Family Liaison Officer, keeping them well informed during the planning process. Duties include the following:    Establish a tentative schedule of events and the length of time the mourning and burial process will involve    Make arrangements for a reception, if so desired by family members    Make arrangements for the surviving family to get from the funeral home to the cemetery    Establish an honor guard schedule to stand guard during the viewing at the funeral home    Arrange for honorary pallbearers    Secure the bugler, color guard(s), band, and firing squad    Obtain an American flag. (The Funeral Director secures a flag for veterans only)    Coordinate any formal walk through of uniformed personnel during the period of viewing with the agencies and Funeral Director. This includes seating arrangements    Coordinate any prayer services to be conducted at the funeral home    Develop a schedule and a brief set of instructions for uniformed personnel the day of the funeral. Be sure to coordinate with the funeral home. UNIFORM POLICY This SOP sets the minimum standards for uniform dress of all Fire Department members. The contents of this SOP are official policy for the purchase, inventory, personal identification, use and wear of all Fire Department uniforms. The standards for proper wear and use of Personal Protective Equipment are contained in SWD 1-0 Protective Equipment/Clothing. The ONLY authorized uniforms shall be those referred to as UNIFORM or OPTIONAL UNIFORM in this SOP. No unauthorized uniform or combination of authorized and unauthorized uniforms shall be allowed. This applies to both Suppression and Division personnel within the Fire Department. Omaha Fire Department uniforms will be classified as A, B, C, D, E, F, or G. STANDARDS OF DRESS Each member's uniform shall be clean and pressed, free of wrinkles, shoes will be shined. Faded, frayed, or torn shirts and trousers will not be acceptable for wear and shall be discarded and replaced as needed or directed. All supervisors are responsible for ensuring that they set a positive example in the wear and maintenance of Fire Department uniforms. Supervisors are responsible for ensuring that assigned employees meet the standards set forth in this instruction. Battalion Chiefs and Captains shall monitor the condition and fit of uniform items worn by personnel under their command. Clothing that is faded out, torn, or does not fit properly shall be replaced. Shirts shall be tucked in at all times. If the company is on special assignment outside of the engine house (i.e., hydrant party, apparatus display), personnel shall wear the Class C, D, or F uniform. With a Battalion Chief’s prior approval, Class G uniform shorts may be worn for a special assignment, but only with a Class F polo shirt. Code Save Recognition Awarded to recognize individuals who have filled roles in the AHA’s Chain of Survival and have directly contributed to a Code Save. These individuals include, but are not limited to, sworn Omaha Fire Department employees, student ride-alongs, bystanders, 911 Dispatchers, other business employees, police officers, etc. Code Save       A patient who was once in cardiac arrest, was resuscitated in the field (ROSC), and discharged home from the hospital. Purpose         To recognize individuals who have filled roles in the AHA’s Chain of Survival and have directly contributed to a Code Save. These individuals include, but are not limited to, sworn Omaha Fire Department employees, student ride- alongs, bystanders, 911 Dispatchers, other business employees, police officers, etc. Criteria for Code Save Recognition 1. The patient was in cardiac arrest (no palpable pulse) at any time before or while under the care of OFD personnel. 2. The patient was successfully resuscitated with a return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC) in the pre-hospital setting. This may occur prior to arrival of OFD personnel. 3. The patient was eventually discharged home from the hospital. It has been determined that if a patient is discharged home, he/she is considered to be neurologically intact. 4. In the event the patient is discharged to a nursing home, the EMS Committee will determine Code Save status based upon reasons for discharge to the specific facility. For example, discharged to a nursing home for physical rehabilitation, etc. Process for Code Save Recognition 1. All Code 99-Resuscitated Code 3 PCRs are reviewed. 2. Follow-up calls to receiving hospital will determine outcomes.
3. PCRs and dialogue w/ OFD personnel will determine who will receive these Code Save Recognitions and what their actions were on the incident. 4. Code Save Recognitions will be handled/reviewed/awarded by the EMS Division Chief and the EMS Committee. Other Goals of the Code Save Recognition 1. Increased dialogue with receiving hospitals regarding patient outcomes and closer review of Code 99 PCRs will potentially help with the OFD QA/QI. 2. Formulate information for ZOLL study. 3. Promote a higher level of patient care through positive reinforcement. 4. Increase the Public Relations aspect of the OFD. AWARDS FOR CIVILIANS Purpose To administer an awards program and give formal recognition to deserving citizens who render aid or assistance to victims of emergencies or who through fire prevention efforts reduce the incidence of fire. Citizen Heroism Award The Citizen Heroism Award may be made for a conspicuous act of valor and heroism by a citizen under hazardous conditions in which the person is placed in a life threatening position to save the life of another. Citizen Lifesaving Award The Citizen Lifesaving Award may be made to a person outside the Fire Department who has been involved in a lifesaving effort at an emergency scene before the arrival of the Fire Department or who has provided extraordinary assistance to Fire Department members in a lifesaving effort. This nomination is normally made be Fire Department personnel assigned to the incident. Certificate of Appreciation A Certificate of Appreciation will be awarded to citizens or organizations that perform an exceptional fire related service to the Department, to a fellow citizen, or to the community. This award consists of a certificate. Policy Any officer may initiate a recommendation to the Fire Chief for recognition of a deserving citizen. This recommendation should be made on OFD Form 34 and forwarded via the chain of
command to the Fire Chief. The OFD Form 34 must include an accurate account of the citizen's action and the class of award recommended. REPORTING, PRESENTATION OF AWARDS, AND AWARDS BOARD Reporting All supervisors shall report any incidents they feel merit consideration on OFD Form 34 and the reporting officer will also recommend a class of award. Recommendations must be accompanied by all supporting documents and endorsed through the chain of command to the Fire Chief. The Board Chairperson or designated alternate will convene the Awards Board monthly, or as needed, to review all recommendations, and, in turn, to recommend to the Fire Chief which awards they find to be appropriate. Presentation of Awards Awards may be presented at a time and place designated by the Fire Chief including daily roll calls, recruit graduations, City Council meetings, or any other appropriate place or time. The time and place will be designated by the Fire Chief. Members receiving an award at formal occasions are to appear in class B uniform. Certificates for the first five classes will be on parchment paper and signed by Mayor, Fire Chief and Chairperson of Awards Board. The sixth class, Department Citation, will be on bond paper. Awards Board Membership The awards committee shall follow the membership as set forth in the current Union contract. The committee will consist of nine members. The Fire Chief will appoint four members with one of these being the Personnel Director or his/her designee. The Union will appoint four members. These eight members will then elect a ninth member.
